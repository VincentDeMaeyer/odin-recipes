Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti is a timeless classic in the world of pasta, loved for its versatility and simplicity. With its long, slender strands, it provides the perfect canvas for a variety of sauces, from rich and savory tomato-based ones to creamy, decadent Alfredo. Originating from Italy, spaghetti has made its way into kitchens around the globe, offering a quick and satisfying meal that can be customized to suit any taste. Whether it's paired with meatballs, vegetables, or seafood, spaghetti remains one of the most beloved pasta shapes, easily adapting to both casual weeknight dinners and special occasions.

The beauty of spaghetti lies in its ability to blend seamlessly with virtually any flavor profile, making it a go-to choice for cooks of all levels. A well-cooked plate of spaghetti is the foundation for countless delicious dishes, making it a staple in kitchens everywhere. The key to perfect spaghetti lies not only in the quality of the pasta itself but also in the way it's prepared: boiled just right, not too soft nor too firm, allowing it to absorb the sauce while maintaining its satisfying texture. In this recipe, we’ll walk through the steps to create a flavorful spaghetti dish that’s sure to impress and satisfy any palate.

ingredients

  • >200g spaghetti (or adjust based on serving size)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
    • ²&

recipe

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions, then drain, reserving a cup of pasta water.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat and sauté the minced garlic for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper to the pan, and simmer the sauce for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Toss the cooked spaghetti into the sauce, adding reserved pasta water as needed to coat the noodles evenly.
  5. Serve the spaghetti topped with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil or parsley, if desired.
